# Optimization Insights

## Scenario Design
The optimization scenario was intentionally constrained to reflect realistic planning conditions:
- **Total budget fixed** at approximately 31M (before and after optimization)
- **Channel movement limits:** -30% to +30% relative to historical spends

This setup answers a practical question: *what can be improved through reallocation alone, without asking for more budget?*

## Performance Comparison
- **Non-optimized incremental revenue:** ~9.38M
- **Optimized incremental revenue:** ~9.49M
- **Estimated lift:** ~+115K incremental revenue

While the lift is moderate, it is achieved under strict constraints and no increase in total investment.

## Recommended Allocation Shifts
- **Digital_Social:** 49% → 53%
- **TV:** 42% → 35%
- **Radio:** 8% → 10%
- **Print:** 2% → 2%

## Interpretation
### 1) Reallocation favors marginal productivity
The recommendation reflects where additional budget is expected to produce better incremental returns, given current response-curve shape and saturation dynamics.

### 2) Lower average ROI does not always mean “cut immediately”
Optimization considers system-level trade-offs and constraints. The objective is portfolio improvement, not isolated channel ranking.

### 3) Scale and efficiency must be balanced
Channels can differ in:
- total contribution capacity,
- cost efficiency,
- available incremental headroom.

A robust allocation strategy balances all three.

## Practical Next Step
Use the optimized mix as a planning baseline, then monitor realized outcomes and refresh MMM periodically to capture market, pricing, and demand shifts.
